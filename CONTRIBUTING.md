# Contributing

Thank you for your interest in VTX Labs. This document explains how to engage
with our open-source projects. It applies to every repository in the
[VTX-Labs](https://github.com/VTX-Labs) organization unless a specific repository
states otherwise.

## How we accept contributions

Our projects are **maintained directly by the VTX Labs team.** To keep a
consistent direction, quality bar, and release cadence across many projects,
**we do not accept unsolicited pull requests.**

That does not mean we don't want your input — community feedback shapes what we
build. The most valuable ways to contribute are:

### Report a bug

Open a **Bug report** issue. A clear, reproducible report — exact version,
steps, expected vs. actual behavior, and environment — is the single most useful
thing you can provide. Use the template; it asks for everything we need.

### Request a feature

Open a **Feature request** issue. Lead with the *problem* you're trying to solve,
then your proposed solution. We prioritize based on impact and fit.

### Improve documentation

Open a **Documentation issue** if something is unclear, missing, outdated, or
incorrect. Pinpoint the location and, where you can, suggest the fix.

### Ask or discuss

For questions, ideas, and open-ended conversation, use
[Discussions](https://github.com/orgs/VTX-Labs/discussions) rather than issues.

## Reporting security vulnerabilities

**Never report a security issue through a public issue or discussion.** Follow
the private disclosure process in [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

## Before you open an issue

- **Search first.** Check [existing issues across the org](https://github.com/search?q=org%3AVTX-Labs&type=issues)
  to avoid duplicates.
- **Use the latest version.** Confirm the issue still occurs on the current release.
- **Reference the project.** Each template asks which repository is affected —
  you can reference any repo in the organization.
- **One issue per report.** Separate, focused issues are easier to triage and resolve.

## Code of conduct

All participation is governed by our [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md). We
expect respectful, constructive communication in every issue and discussion.
